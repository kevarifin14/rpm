George was successful in his goal of architecting a fully functional platform for managing a portfolio of solar assets. However, he saw that this platform’s scalability was limited by the tools and manual processes that it relied upon. As SolarCity’s customer base approached 100,000 contracts and executive leaders set targets to reach half a million within the next few years, it became apparent to George that a software solution was required in order for financial operations to efficiently scale with the business.


Founder & CEO

George Zviagin

George founded Ra Power Management to lower the cost of capital for solar project financing by providing a software platform that empowers distributed solar investors and developers to efficiently scale the management of their data and workflows. Prior to RPM, George was a Portfolio Manager at SolarCity where he led the Portfolio Management group. While at SolarCity, George managed the company’s $4+ billion / 750+ MW portfolio of solar assets and directed investor relations with all funds, lenders and limited partners. Additionally, he was a leading contributor to the historic and successful issuance of solar’s first two rounds of asset backed securitization (ABS).

Co-Founder & COO

Aaron Iverson

Aaron first realized solar’s potential while working on a research project for Harrington Ventures LLC, a real estate investment fund in the San Francisco bay area. Although he saw many possible opportunities in the solar space, it wasn’t until he began discussing the idea of a solar asset management platform with George did everything click. With over 10 years of experience working with a variety of asset management systems, it was easy for him to see the power and value in an integrated platform tailored specifically for solar. With George’s extensive knowledge of industry best practices and Aaron’s first-hand experience working with some of the best asset management systems on the market, the two came together to form RPM.
